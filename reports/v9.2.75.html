Summary

Release v9.2.75 is a small release of 4 changes. The one customer-facing highlight is self-serve payment dispute management in Banking (BAN-348), which lets property managers review, draft, and submit evidence for Stripe payment disputes directly inside DoorLoop instead of routing through the risk team. The remaining three items are internal hardening: a new background queue that makes voice post-call transcription/summary more reliable, scaffolding for a new communications-purchases library, and a rent-reminder performance optimization. Only the dispute feature has Help Center impact — and it's a content gap (no existing article covers disputes).

PMO-484 Scaffold communications-purchases library Added idempotent one-time purchase tracking with pending, completed, and failed statuses for clearer billing reconciliation. Despite the product-sounding Slack note, this ticket (SP-1) only scaffolds an empty, properly-configured Nx library (@doorloop/communications-purchases) with boundary rules — the actual purchase model and service land in later tickets. PMO-1028 Voice post-call processing → BullMQ queue Added background processing for voice call follow-up tasks so recordings can be transcribed, summarized, and published more reliably. Moves voice post-call work (transcribe → AI summary → publish) off a fire-and-forget promise onto a dedicated, retry-aware background queue so a worker crash no longer silently loses a call's transcript and summary. Reliability improvement to the (beta) Voice pipeline. INTERNAL — Backend reliability/NFR; no Help Center impact. Reduces missing-transcript incidents in Voice.

BAN-348 Self-serve payment dispute management Added dispute management so users can view, edit, save drafts, and submit evidence from payment transactions. When a transaction is in DISPUTED status, the status chip becomes clickable and a "View Dispute" action opens an embedded Stripe dispute UI scoped to that charge. PMs can review dispute details, upload/save draft evidence, and submit responses without leaving DoorLoop — removing the risk team from the loop.
